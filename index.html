How to Play

Everything you need to know — including how skeletal drawing works

// What is a structural isomer?

Structural (constitutional) isomers share the same molecular formula but have different connectivity. Butane (C₄H₁₀) can be a straight chain or branched — same four carbons, completely different structure. This game challenges you to find every valid connectivity for a given formula.

// Bond-line (skeletal) drawing

In skeletal notation, carbons are implicit at every vertex and endpoint of a zigzag line. You only write labels for heteroatoms (O, N, Cl…) and for H when attached to a heteroatom. This is the standard way chemists draw structures.

01

Draw a bond

Click and drag anywhere on the canvas. The line snaps to 60° angles. Release to place the bond. Carbon is implicit at each endpoint.

02

Extend a chain

Drag starting from any existing vertex to continue the chain. The snap keeps your structure in standard zigzag form.

03

Double bonds

Click the "Double" button then draw. A second parallel line appears automatically alongside the first.

04

Add heteroatoms

Select O, N, Cl etc. from the toolbar. When you drag to a vertex or draw to a new point, that endpoint becomes the chosen atom instead of C.

05

Rings

Draw bonds in a closed loop — end your drag on an existing vertex to close the ring. Cyclic structures are valid isomers.

06

Check & score

Click "Check Structure" to validate formula, valence, connectivity and uniqueness. Each new valid isomer = 10 pts.

// Validation rules

  • Formula match — implicit H is calculated from unused valence. No need to draw H's on carbons.
  • Connected graph — all atoms must form a single connected structure.
  • Valence limits — C(4), O(2), N(3), H(1), halogens(1), S(2). Overvalent atoms are flagged.
  • Uniqueness — graph canonicalization detects duplicate connectivity even if drawn at a different angle or position.
  • No stereoisomers — cis/trans and R/S are the same structure in this game.

// Strategy tips

  • Alkanes: Systematically shorten the longest chain by one and add a methyl branch.
  • Degrees of unsaturation: Each ring or double bond costs one DoU. C₄H₈ has 1 DoU — could be a ring or an alkene.
  • Heteroatom position: For O-containing formulas, try every position: terminal (alcohol/aldehyde), interior (ether/ketone).
  • Rings count: Don't forget cyclic isomers — cyclobutane and but-1-ene are both C₄H₈.
  • Snap help: The 60° snap keeps structures tidy. Bonds ≥ 30px register; short accidental drags are ignored.
